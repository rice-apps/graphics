* We are aggressively looking for team leads! If you would like to nominate anyone, please tell them about us, or email xal1@rice.edu!

Team leads are responsible for the growth of the app by deciding which features need to be added to their project and delegating them to developers. They are also the point of contact for the project.

There is only one waitlist for all team leads. You can get bumped to the top of the waitlist by emailing xal1@rice.edu for an early interview. All team leads will have a short interview about his or her skills.

Official Responsibilities:

  • Directing the growth of your app
  • Delegating tasks for team members
  • Presenting at the Rice Apps Showcase at the end of the year
  • Attending one of the three coding parties per week
  • Ensuring the entire team attends the same coding party
  • Mentoring inexperienced team members if needed
  • Relaying information from the officers to your team and vice versa
  • Acting as a representative and point of contact for your app
  • Code reviewing all pull requests and merging onto master branch
  • Reviewing submitted minitasks

Developers are the primary workforce for all the projects. Tasks will be assigned to each developer at a weekly meeting, which should be completed by the next meeting. Developers can work on a variety of areas, such as frontend, mobile development, backend, etc. Whatever you're interested in!

There is a separate waitlist for each project for developers. You can complete any project's minitasks to get bumped to the top of the waitlist of that project. Please see the Minitask tab for details.

Official Responsibilities:

  • Contributing ideas to the growth of your app
  • Completing tasks delegated to you by the team lead
  • Presenting at the Rice Apps Showcase at the end of the year
  • Attending one of the three coding parties per week

Designers work together as a team to help out with UI/UX for all projects. Each designer is assigned to a team as a point of contact for the team lead, but all the designers work together to create the frontend for the project. The graphics design to HTML/CSS/JavaScript coding ratio is about 35:65. But as always, you're free to focus on whatever you like!

There is only one waitlist for all designers. You can get bumped to the top of the waitlist by submitting examples of your visual/web designs to xal1@rice.edu.

Official Responsibilities:

  • Create relevant visual graphics
  • Design a working UX layout for projects
  • Collaborating with the other designers
  • Attending one of the three coding parties per week with designers
  • Attending the coding party for the team you are assigned to
  • Publicizing newly released apps

Applications are accepted on a rolling basis. Your position on the waitlist is almost solely determined by the timestamp of your application submission. However, completing minitasks shows us that you're a motivated developer and will bump you to the top of the waitlist. Click the positions on the left to learn more about them!

* Minitask descriptions and instructions have not been added yet. Please check back soon!

A minitask is similar to the tasks that developers normally complete every week. However, they are simplified so that most COMP 140/182 students should be able to complete the tasks, if they Google smartly. Once you complete a task, send your code to the team lead. He or she will review the code and either immediately accept you or bump you to the top of that project's waitlist.

Current Minitasks:

  • Wellbeing - Create the scene for the alcohol shots calculator
  • Wellbeing - Create the marker for the Night Escort bus
  • Roommate Finder - Mitigating security vulnerabilities
  • Roommate Finder - Validating a string's format
  • Roommate Finder - Determining the type of device visiting the site
  • Petition App - Send an email after a certain count
